[![Yahoo! engineer Chad Auld at YUICONF 2009, held at the Yahoo! HQ in Sunnyvale; October 28, 2009.](/yuiblog/blog-archive/assets/auld-yuiconf2009-phploader-20091103-080850.jpg)](http://developer.yahoo.com/yui/theater/video.php?v=auld-yuiconf2009-phploader)

[Chad Auld](http://opensourcepenguin.net/) ([@chadauld](http://twitter.com/chadauld)) is a Yahoo! engineer best known for his work on [MiaCMS](http://miacms.org/) and [Sideline](http://sideline.yahoo.com). Chad recently [drove the open-source release](/yuiblog/blog/2009/09/14/yui-php-loader-beta-release/) of [YUI's PHP Loader](http://developer.yahoo.com/yui/phploader/) (taking over from original developer Adam Moore \[[@admo](http://twitter.com/admo)\]), and he came to [YUICONF 2009](http://yuilibrary.com/yuiconf2009/) last week to talk about the project and provide an introduction to its core features. PHP Loader provides a generic, flexible loading mechanism for modular JavaScript/CSS projects, and while it ships with [YUI 2](http://developer.yahoo.com/yui/2/) and [YUI 3](http://developer.yahoo.com/yui/3/) metadata it can be used for any frontend project written in PHP. Chad's talk gave the audience a unique opportunity to hear about this utility directly from its chief coder.

If the video embed below doesn't show up correctly in your RSS reader of choice, be sure to [click through to watch the high-resolution version of the video on YUI Theater](http://developer.yahoo.com/yui/theater/video.php?v=auld-yuiconf2009-phploader); the downloadable version is much smaller, optimized as it is for iPods, iPhones, and other handheld devices.
